# Profile

Your profile is how you are seen from a distance. Before anyone works with you, they'll look you up. Every opportunity stands behind a Google search.

**You are who Google says you are.**

If nothing comes up — low chance of a big deal. If it's confusing — low chance of a deal. If it's clear you're a respected, known, trusted KPI — high chance they want to work with you.

## The SALT Framework

### S — Social Media Accounts
At least 4 platforms where you show up regularly. Consistent presence, consistent messaging, consistent brand.

### A — Awards & Associations
Win awards in your industry. Associate yourself with brands people already recognise. These are credibility signals that work from a distance.

### L — Live Events
Speak at events. Host events. Sponsor events. Attend events. A huge amount of real business happens in person. KPIs are visible at live events.

### T — Third-Party Platforms
Appear on other people's podcasts. Get featured on other platforms. Be invited onto traditional media. When other people put you on their stage, it signals that you're worth paying attention to.

## Becoming the Spotlight, Not Chasing It

Building a profile is not about saying "look at me." It's about saying **"look at this."**

You're not showcasing your breakfast or your car. You're showcasing your ideas, your work, and your clients' success.

The goal is not to chase the spotlight. It's to become the spotlight — the person others point to when they want to highlight great work in your industry.

## The Google Test

Google yourself. What comes up? That's your current profile. Now ask: what do I want it to look like in 12 months?
