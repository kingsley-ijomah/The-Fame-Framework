# Your Core Message

Before you set up platforms, create content, or build products, you need to know what you stand for. Your core message is the foundation everything else is built on.

## The Social Pitch

Your core message lives inside your social pitch — a 30-45 second statement that tells people:

- **Name** — Who you are
- **Same** — What industry/space you operate in (so they can place you)
- **Fame** — What you're known for or becoming known for
- **Aim** — What you're working towards or what you help people achieve
- **Game** — How you do it / your unique approach

When someone hears your social pitch, the reaction you want is: *"You're up to something. I want to know more."*

## Why This Comes First

Your social pitch informs:
- Your bios on every platform
- The content you create
- The products you build
- How you introduce yourself at events
- Who you attract as partners

Get this wrong (or skip it) and everything downstream is scattered.

## How to Develop It

Don't try to perfect it on day one. Write a first draft, use it in real conversations, and refine it based on how people react. You'll know it's working when people lean in and ask follow-up questions.
