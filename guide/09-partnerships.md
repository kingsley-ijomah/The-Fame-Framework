# Partnerships

Partnerships are where key people of influence team up with other KPIs. Done right, 1 + 1 = 11. This is the multiplier that takes everything to another level.

**Important:** Only KPIs can drive partnerships effectively. This is not something you delegate. You, as the founder, have to lead this. And you need the other 4 Ps in decent shape before partnerships will work — nobody partners with someone invisible.

## Three Types of Partnerships

### Brand Partnerships
Partner with someone more well-known than you. Their credibility enhances yours by association.

*Example: Nike partners with Serena Williams. Nespresso partners with George Clooney.*

### Product Partnerships
Combine products that complement each other. One product enhances the other.

*Example: Porsche includes Bose stereos and Pirelli tyres. The products are better together.*

### Distribution Partnerships
Get your products in front of more people through someone else's channels and audience.

*Example: Getting stocked in Walmart. Being included in an airline's offering.*

## The Opportunity

Somebody woke up this morning with everything you've ever wanted — and they're looking for a partnership:

- Someone has money and could invest
- Someone has expertise and could join your advisory board
- Someone has a million email subscribers and could run a campaign with you

Great partnerships solve problems you can't solve alone. You just need to know how to find and structure them.

## When You're Ready

Don't rush into partnerships before the other 4 Ps are solid. Partnerships amplify what's already working — they can't fix what isn't.
