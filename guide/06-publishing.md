# Publishing

Publishing means taking your ideas and putting them into the public domain. The word "publish" means to make public.

There's a reason "author" and "authority" are so closely linked. When you become an author, you become an authority. Those who publish, scale.

## What Counts as Publishing

- Videos
- Podcasts
- Blog posts and articles
- Social media content
- Books
- Reports and guides
- Newsletters

A book is the gold standard — but all formats count. The key is that your ideas are out there, accessible, and working for you even when you're not in the room.

## Prolific Beats Perfect

Do not try to be a perfectionist. Be a **directionalist** — someone who moves directionally correct with everything they publish.

The Beatles wrote roughly 400 songs. Only about 30-40 became number ones. They're still the biggest selling band ever.

You'll publish some things that are world-class. You'll publish some things that are cringe. The world-class stuff gets seen. The cringe stuff never takes off anyway. So it doesn't matter.

## The Practice

- Set a publishing cadence you can sustain (daily, 3x/week, weekly)
- Prioritise consistency over quality in the early stages
- Repurpose across platforms — one idea can become a video, a post, a newsletter
- Track your output volume, not just engagement
