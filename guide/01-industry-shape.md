# The Industry Shape

## The Power Law

In every industry, income follows the same pattern. The vast majority of people earn between 50K and 150K. But once you reach the top 5%, incomes go exponential — 600K, 1M, 1.5M and beyond.

This isn't a bell curve. It's a power law. And technology is accelerating it.

## Three Categories of People

Every industry has three groups:

**Newbies** — Fresh, excited, still learning. Their currency is skills. They're trying to get good enough to become workers.

**Worker Bees** — Skilled and paid. They know the realities of the industry. Plenty of opportunities, but those opportunities pay 50-150K. This is where most people stay.

**Key People of Influence (KPIs)** — A small group who are the most connected, most visible personal brands. They earn exponentially more than everyone else.

## The Merry-Go-Round of Distraction

Here's the trap: a worker bee in Industry A sees a key person of influence in Industry B and thinks "I want that." So they jump to Industry B — and start over as a newbie.

They grind back up to worker bee, get disillusioned, spot another KPI in Industry C, and jump again. Newbie → Worker Bee → Newbie → Worker Bee. Round and round.

Every "amazing opportunity" they chase is shielded by an army of newbies and worker bees attracted to the same thing.

## The Way Out

Stop jumping. Pick the industry you love. Commit to becoming the key person of influence in *that* industry.

The functional skills that made you a worker bee won't make you a KPI. You need five new skills — the 5 Ps:

**Pitching → Publishing → Products → Profile → Partnerships**

That's what the rest of this guide is about.
