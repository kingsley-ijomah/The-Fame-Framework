# Shadow a KPI

Before you set up your platforms and infrastructure, find someone who's already where you want to be. Study them. Reverse-engineer what's working.

This isn't about copying. It's about using a proven playbook as your starting reference so you're not guessing in the dark.

## Why This Works

Choosing platforms, content formats, and messaging from scratch is overwhelming. But someone in your industry has already figured it out. They've tested what works. You can see the results.

By studying 1-2 KPIs in your industry, you shortcut months of trial and error in the Prep phase.

## Who to Pick

Look for someone who:

- Is a clear key person of influence in your industry (or a closely related one)
- Has the kind of visibility and positioning you want
- Is active and current — not someone who peaked years ago
- You genuinely respect and admire

Pick 1-2 people. No more. You're studying, not collecting.

## What to Map

For each KPI, research and document:

### Their Platforms
- Which social media platforms are they on?
- Where are they most active?
- Which platform seems to drive the most engagement for them?

### Their Content
- What formats do they use? (video, written, audio, live)
- How often do they publish?
- What topics and themes come up repeatedly?
- What's their tone and style?

### Their Pitch
- How do they introduce themselves? (bio, about page, video intros)
- What's their core message?
- What makes people lean in?

### Their Product Ecosystem
- What do they give away for free? (gift)
- What's their low-cost entry product? (prospect)
- What's their flagship offering? (core)
- Do they have an ongoing membership or subscription? (client)

### Their Profile (SALT)
- Which social accounts are active?
- What awards or associations do they have?
- Do they speak at or host live events?
- Where do they appear as a guest? (podcasts, media, other platforms)

### Their Partnerships
- Who are they partnered with?
- What type — brand, product, or distribution?

### Their Google Result
- Google their name. What comes up?
- What's the overall impression from a distance?

## What to Do With This

You're not cloning them. You're answering: *"What infrastructure and approach is proven in my industry?"*

Then in the Prep phase, you make informed decisions:
- Use similar platforms (because that's where your audience is)
- Adopt content formats that work (because the audience responds to them)
- Structure your product ecosystem with a proven model in mind
- Set a profile benchmark you can work towards
