# Pitching

Pitching is the ability to enroll people into new ideas. It's how you get someone who wasn't thinking about your idea to become excited about it.

Being a key person of influence is the journey of a thousand pitches. The person who pitches best becomes the KPI.

## Three Types of Pitches

### Social Pitch (30-45 seconds)
Used in social media and casual settings. Covers your name, same, fame, aim, and game. The goal: make them want to know more.

### Scheduled Pitch (15 minutes to 2 hours)
A prepared presentation for a specific person or audience. You know who they are, they know who you are. Your goal: move them from Path A to Path B — get them to take a specific course of action.

### Sales Pitch (transactional)
Designed to close. Either selling a product or securing an investment. This is about achieving a transaction.

## You Get What You Pitch For

You're always pitching — whether you realise it or not. Negative people pitch negativity into existence. Intentional people pitch their vision into existence.

Be deliberate about what you pitch. If you pitch problems, you'll attract problems. If you pitch possibility, you'll attract opportunity.

## How to Get Better

- Write your social pitch and practise it until it's natural
- Prepare tailored scheduled pitches before every important meeting
- Track how many pitches you make weekly — volume matters
- Pay attention to what makes people lean in vs. tune out
- Refine constantly
